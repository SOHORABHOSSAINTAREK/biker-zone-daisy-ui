Frequently Asked Questions

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If your helmet is visibly cracked, damaged, or has been involved in an impact, it's likely time to replace it. Additionally, if your helmet is more than 5 years old, it's a good idea to get a new one, as the materials may degrade over time, compromising its effectiveness in protecting your head.

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

Is it safe to ride my bike that has been in storage for some time?

Always wear a properly fitted helmet and follow traffic laws, signals, and signs to ensure safe cycling. Stay aware of your surroundings, signal your intentions, and use bike lanes or paths whenever possible.

"How can I ensure safe bike riding on national highways?"

Avoid riding a bike on national highways as they are designed for high-speed motor vehicles. If it's necessary to cross or use them, dismount and walk your bike across, or find alternative routes that prioritize cyclist safety.

"How can I acquire the necessary permit for international bike travel?"

To acquire the necessary permit for international bike travel, you should contact the relevant authorities or embassies of the countries you plan to visit and follow their application procedures and requirements. It's essential to ensure you have all the required documentation in order to travel legally and smoothly.
